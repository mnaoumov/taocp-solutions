First summand: $\frac{1}{1 \times 2}$

Last summand: $\frac{1}{(n-1) \times n}$

This makes sense, only if we start sum from $n = 2$, not from $n = 1$ as it mistakenly written in the proof.

This way the sum will be $1 - \frac{1}{n}$, and the proof holds.
