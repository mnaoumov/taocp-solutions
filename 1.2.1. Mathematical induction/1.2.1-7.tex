\( 1^2 = 1 = \frac{1(1+1)}{2} \)

\( 2^2 - 1^2 = 4-1=3=\frac{2(2+1)}{2} \)

\( 3^2-2^2+1^2=9-4+1=6=\frac{3(3+1)}{2} \)

\( 4^2-3^2+2^2-1^2=16-9+4-1=10=\frac{4(4+1)}{2} \)

Let's prove \( P(n): A(n) = n^2 - (n-1)^2 + (n-2)^2 + ... \pm 1^2 = \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \)

\( P(1) \) checked

Assume \( P(1), ..., P(n-1) \) hold.

\( A(n) = n^2 - A(n-1) =n^2 - \frac{(n-1)n}{2}=\frac{2n^2-n^2+n}{2}=\frac{n(n+1)}{2} \implies P(n) \)

\textbf{Q.E.D.}
