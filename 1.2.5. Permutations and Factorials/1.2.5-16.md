# 1.2.5-16

${} n! = \sum_{m \geq 0}(\sum_{k=0}^m \frac{(-1)^k}{k!})\prod_{j=0}^{m-1}(n-j) {}$

Let ${} m \geq n + 3 {}$

${} |\prod_{j=0}^{m-1}(n-j)| = |n-m+1| \cdot \prod_{j=0}^{m-2}|n-j| = |n-m+1| \cdot \prod_{0 \leq j \leq n} |n-j| \cdot \prod_{n + 1 \leq j \leq m - 2} |n-j| = {}$

${} = (m-n-1) \cdot \overbrace{\prod_{0 \leq j \leq n} (n-j)}^A \cdot \prod_{n + 1 \leq j \leq m - 2} (j - n) \geq (m-n-1) \cdot A \cdot \prod_{n + 1 \leq j \leq m - 2} 1 = {}$

${} = (m-n-1) \cdot A {}$

${} n \notin \mathbb{N} \implies A > 0 {}$

${} (m-n-1)A \to \infty, m\to\infty {}$

${} \sum_{k=0}^m \frac{(-1)^k}{k!} \to \frac{1}{e}, m \to \infty {}$

${} \sum_{k=0}^m \frac{(-1)^k}{k!})\prod_{j=0}^{m-1}(n-j) \not\to 0, m \to \infty {}$
