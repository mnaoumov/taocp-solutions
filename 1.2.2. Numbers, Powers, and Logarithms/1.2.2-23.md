# 1.2.2-23

${} \ln{xy} = S(F_{1,xy}) {}$ - area under the plot from ${} 1 {}$ to ${} xy {}$

${} S(F_{1,xy}) = S(F_{1x}) + S(F_{x,xy}) {}$

Let's transform ${} F_{x,xy} {}$ preserving it's area. We shrink horizonal axis `x` times and expand vertical axis `x` times.

If original coordinates were ${} (u,v) {}$

${} u'=\frac{u}{x}, v'=vx {}$

The curve in old coordinates ${} (u,v) {}$ was ${} uv = 1 {}$.

${} u'v'=uv=1 \implies {}$ In new coordinates the curve stays the same

${} F_{x,xy} \to F'_{x,xy} = F_{1y} {}$

${} \implies \ln{xy} = \ln{x} + \ln{y} {}$
