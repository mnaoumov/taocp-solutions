# 1.2.2-6

${} m = n, \forall k \geq 1: d_k = e_k \implies x = y {}$

${} m < n {}$ or ${} m = n, \exists k \geq 1 \forall j, 1 \leq j < k: d_j = e_j, d_k < e_k \implies x < y {}$

${} m > n {}$ or ${} m = n, \exists k \geq 1 \forall j, 1 \leq j < k: d_j = e_j, d_k > e_k \implies x > y {}$
