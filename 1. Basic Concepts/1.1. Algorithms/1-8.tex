\( (m, n) \to a^{m}b^{n} \).

The idea is to \( (m, n) \to (|m - n|, \min(m, n)) \).

\subsubsection*{1.1.}

To find \( | m - n | \) we can remove middle \( ab \) until they all gone. We will be left with

\begin{itemize}
\item \( a^{m-n} \), if \( m > n \).
\item \( \varepsilon \), empty word, if \( m = n \).
\item \( b^{n-m} \), if \( m < n \).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{1.2.}

If we then replace all \( b \) with \( a \), we will get \( a^{|m-n|} \).

However, with this approach, we lost \( \min(m, n) \), which is exactly the amount of removals in 1.1.

So we need another approach.

\subsubsection*{2.1}

We need to replace \( ab \) with some additional character \( c \).

\( a^{m}b^{n} \to a^{m-1}cb^{n-1} \)

\subsubsection*{2.2}

Then we need to move \( c \) to the end. Via repeating replacement \( cb \to bc \).

\( a^{m-1}cb^{n-1} \to a^{m-1}bcb^{n-2} \to \cdots \to a^{m-1}b^{n-1}c \)

Then we go to 2 and repeat. We will get

\begin{itemize}
\item \( a^{m-n}c^{n} \), if \( m > n \)
\item \( c^{n} \), if \( m = n \)
\item \( b^{n-m}c^{m} \), if \( m < n \)
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{2.3.}

Then replace all \( b \to a \).

\subsubsection*{2.4.}

Then replace all \( c \to b \).

And we get \( a^{|m-n|}b^{\min(m,n)} \).

Go to \textbf{2.1}.

\subsubsection*{2.5.}

When we get \( a^{m} \), \( m \) --- is a desired gcd of original \( m \) and \( n \).

\subsubsection*{Final solution}

\( A := \{ a, b, c \} \)

\( N := 5 \)

\( \theta_{0} := b \)

\( \phi_{0} := b \)

\( a_{0} := 5, (a^{m}, 0) \to (a^{m}, 5) \)

\( b_{0} := 1, (a^{m}b^{n}, 0) \to (a^{m}b^{n}, 1) \)

\( \theta_{1} := ab \)

\( \phi_{1} := c \)

\( a_{1} := 3, (a^{m}c^{p}, 1) \to (a^{m}c^{p}, 3), (b^{n}c^{p}, 1) \to (b^{n}c^{p}, 3) \)

\( b_{1} := 2, (a^{m}b^{n}c^{p}, 1) \to (a^{m-1}cb^{n-1}c^{p}, 2) \)

\( \theta_{2} = cb \)

\( \phi_{2} = bc \)

\( a_{2} = 1, (a^{m}b^{n}c^{p}, 2) \to (a^{m}b^{n}c^{p}, 1) \)

\( b_{2} = 2, (a^{m}b^{n}cb^{p}c^{q}, 2) \to (a^{m}b^{n+1}cb^{p-1}c^{q}, 2) \)

\( \theta_{3} = b \)

\( \phi_{3} = a \)

\( a_{3} = 4, (a^{m}c^{p}, 3) \to (a^{m}c^{p}, 4) \)

\( b_{3} = 3, (a^{m}b^{n}c^{p}, 3) \to (a^{m + 1}b^{n-1}c^{p}, 3) \)

\( \theta_{4} = c \)

\( \phi_{4} = b \)

\( a_{4} = 0, (a^{m}b^{n}, 4) \to (a^{m}b^{n}, 0) \)

\( b_{4} = 4, (a^{m}b^{n}c^{p}, 4) \to (a^{m}b^{n+1}c^{p-1}, 4) \)

\( \theta_{5} = \varepsilon \)

\( \phi_{i} = \varepsilon \)

\( a_{i} = 5 \), never happens

\( b_{i} = 5, (a^{m}, 5) \to (a^{m}, 5) \)
