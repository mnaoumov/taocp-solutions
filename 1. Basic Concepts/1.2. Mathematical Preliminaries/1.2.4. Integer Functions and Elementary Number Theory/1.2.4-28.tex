\textbf{Lemma} \( a \perp n, b \perp n \implies (ab) \perp n \)

Let \( d = \gcd(ab, n) \)

Assume \( d > 1 \implies \exists p \) --- prime, \( p \backslash d \implies p \backslash (ab), p \backslash n \)

\( a \perp n = 1 \implies a \perp p = 1 \)

\( ab \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \)

\( \implies b \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \implies p \backslash b \implies p \backslash \gcd(b,n) \implies b \not\perp n \) --- Contradiction

\( 0 \leq x_{1} < x_{2} < \cdots < x_{\varphi(m)} \leq m - 1, x_{k} \perp m, 1 \leq k \leq \varphi(m) \)

\( y_{k} := (ax_{k}) \mod m, 1 \leq k \leq \varphi(m) \)

\( a \perp m, x_{k} \perp m \overset{\text{Lemma}}{\implies} (ax_{k}) \perp m \implies y_{k} \perp m \)

\( y_{k} = y_{l} \implies ax_{k} \equiv ax_{l} \ (\mathrm{modulo}\ m) \implies x_{k} \equiv x_{l}\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \implies x_{k} = x_{l} \)

\( k \neq l \implies y_{k} \neq y_{l} \)

\( \implies \{ y_{1}, y_{2}, \ldots, y_{\varphi(m)} \} = \{ x_{1}, x_{2}, \ldots, x_{\varphi(m)} \} \)
