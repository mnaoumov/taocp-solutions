\( P(n) \iff \{ n > 1, n\, \) is a product of prime numbers \( \} \)

\( P(1) \) holds automatically

Assume \( P(k) \) holds for all \( k < n \)

If \( n \) is prime, then \( n = n \) is a product of prime numbers and \( P(n) \) holds.

If \( n \) is not prime, then \( n = ab, a, b > 1 \implies a, b < n \implies P(a), P(b) \)

\( a = p_{1}p_{2} \cdot\ \cdots\ \cdot p_{k} \)

\( b = q_{1}q_{2} \cdot\ \cdots\ \cdot q_{l} \)

\( \implies n = ab = p_{1}p_{2} \cdot\ \cdots\ \cdot p_{k} q_{1}q_{2} \cdot\ \cdots\ \cdot q_{l} \) --- product of primes \( \implies P(n) \)

\textbf{Q.E.D.}
