Choose \( s \in \mathbb{N}_{0}: s^{m} \leq x < {(s+1)}^{m} \)

\( {(s + \frac{d_{1}}{10})}^{m} \leq x < {(s + \frac{d_{1}}{10} + \frac{1}{10})}^{m} \)

\( {(s + \frac{d_{1}}{10} + \frac{d_{2}}{100})}^{m} \leq x < {(s + \frac{d_{1}}{10} + \frac{d_{2}}{100} + \frac{1}{100})}^{m} \)

\( \cdots \)

\( {(s + \frac{d_{1}}{10} + \frac{d_{2}}{100} + \cdots + \frac{d_{k}}{10^{k}})}^{m} \leq x < {(s + \frac{d_{1}}{10} + \frac{d_{2}}{100} + \cdots + \frac{d_{k}}{10^{k}} + \frac {1}{10^{k}})}^{m} \)

That's how we get the number \( y = s + \frac{d_{1}}{10} + \frac{d_{2}}{100} + \cdots + \frac{d_{k}}{10^{k}} + \cdots \)

Let \( y_{k} := s + \frac{d_{1}}{10} + \frac{d_{2}}{100} + \cdots + \frac{d_{k}}{10^{k}} \)

\[y_{k}^{m} \leq x < {(y_{k} + \frac{1}{10^{k}})}^{m} \tag{1}\label{eq:1}\]

\( y = \lim_{k \to \infty} y_{k} \)

Take limit in~\eqref{eq:1}

\( y^{m} \leq x \leq y^{m} \implies y^{m} = x \)

\( y < z \implies x = y^{m} < z^{m} \implies z \neq x \)

\( y > z \implies x = y^{m} > z^{m} \implies z \neq x \)

\( y \) --- unique, such that \( y^{m} = x \)
