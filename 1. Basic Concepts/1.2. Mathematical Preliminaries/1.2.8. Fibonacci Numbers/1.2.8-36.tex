\section{Length}\label{sec:length}

\( S_1 = \text{"a"}, S_2 = \text{"b"}, S_{n+2} = S_{n+1}S_n, n \geq 1 \)

\( l(S_1) = 1 = F_1 \)

\( l(S_2) = 1 = F_2 \)

\( l(S_{n+2}) = l(S_{n+1}) + l(S_n) = F_{n+1} + F_n = F_{n+2} \)

\( \implies l(S_n) = F_n \)

\section{Count of "a" and "b"}\label{sec:count}

Let \( a_n - \) amount of \text{"a"} in \( S_n \).

\( b_n - \) amount of \text{"b"} in \( S_n \).

\( a_1 = 1 = F_{-1} \)

\( a_2 = 0 = F_{0} \)

\( a_{n+2} = a_{n+1} + a_n \)

\( \implies a_n = F_{n-2} \)

\( b_1 = 0 = F_{0} \)

\( b_2 = 1 = F_{1} \)

\( b_{n+2} = b_{n+1} + b_n \)

\( \implies b_n = F_{n-1} \)

\section{Density of "b"}\label{sec:density}

\( db_n = \frac{b_n}{l(S_n)} = \frac{F_{n-1}}{F_n} \to \phi^{-1}, n \to \infty \)

\section{First letter}\label{sec:first-letter}

For \( n \geq 2: S_n[1] = \text{"b"} \).

\( n = 2: S_2[1] = \text{"b"}[1] = \text{"b"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:first-letter})

\( \forall n \geq 3: S_n[1] = (S_{n-1}S_{n-2})[1] = S_{n-1}[1] = \text{"b"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:first-letter})

\section{Last letter}\label{sec:last-letter}

\( S_n[F_n] = \text{"a"} \iff 2 \not| n \).

Consider \( c_k := S_{2k-1}[F_{2k-1}], d_k := S_{2k}[F_{2k}] \)

Let's prove \( c_k = \text{"a"}, d_k = \text{"b"} \).

\( c_1 = S_1[1] = \text{"a"}[1] = \text{"a"} \)

\( c_{k+1} = S_{2k+1}[F_{2k+1}] = (S_{2k}S_{2k-1})[F_{2k+1}] = S_{2k-1}[F_{2k-1}] = c_k = \text{"a"} \)

\( d_1 = S_2[1] = \text{"b"}[1] = \text{"b"} \)

\( d_{k+1} = S_{2k+2}[F_{2k+2}] = (S_{2k+1}S_{2k})[F_{2k+2}] = S_{2k}[F_{2k}] = d_k = \text{"b"} \)

\section{No "aa"}\label{sec:no-aa}

No "aa" appears in \( S_n \).

\( n = 1: S_1 = \text{"a"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-aa})

\( n = 2: S_2 = \text{"b"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-aa})

\( n = 3: S_3 = \text{"ba"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-aa})

\( n \geq 4: S_n = S_{n-1}S_{n-2} \)

By inductive assumption, \( S_{n-1} \) and \( S_{n-2} \) do not contain "aa".

The only possible way to get "aa" in \( S_n \) is to have "a" at the end of \( S_{n-1} \) and "a" at the beginning of \( S_{n-2} \).

But \( n - 2 \geq 2 \implies S_{n-2} \) starts with "b".

\( \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-aa})

\section{New "bb"}\label{sec:new-bb}

New "bb" appears in \( S_n \) for \( n = 3 + 2k, k \geq 1 \).

New "bb" appears in \( S_n \) if and only if \( S_{n-1} \) ends with "b" and \( S_{n-2} \) starts with "b".

\( S_{n-1} \) ends with "b" \( \iff 2 | (n-1) \)

\( S_{n-2} \) starts with "b" \( \iff n - 2 \geq 2 \)

\( \implies \) (\ref{sec:new-bb})

\section{Letter "a" at position \( k \)}\label{sec:a-at-k}

\( S_n[k] = \text{"a"} \iff k - 1 = F_{m_1} + F_{m_2} + \cdots + F_{m_r}, m_1 \gg m_2 \gg \cdots \gg m_r = 2 \)

\( S_n[k] = S_{m_r+1}[F_{m_r}+1] = (S_{m_r}S_{m_r-1})[F_{m_r}+1] = S_{m_r-1}[1] \)

\( S_{m_r-1}[1] = \text{"a"} \iff m_r-1 = 1 \iff m_r = 2 \)

\section{No starting "bb"}\label{sec:no-starting-bbb}

No starting "bb" appears in \( S_n \).

\( n = 1: S_1 = \text{"a"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-starting-bbb})

\( n = 2: S_2 = \text{"b"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-starting-bbb})

\( n = 3: S_3 = \text{"ba"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-starting-bbb})

\( n \geq 4: S_n[1..2] = (S_{n-1}S_{n-2})[1..2] = S_{n-1}[1..2] \neq \text{"bb"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-starting-bbb})

\section{No ending "bb"}\label{sec:no-ending-bbb}

No ending "bb" appears in \( S_n \).

\( n = 1: S_1 = \text{"a"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-ending-bbb})

\( n = 2: S_2 = \text{"b"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-ending-bbb})

\( n = 3: S_3 = \text{"ba"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-ending-bbb})

\( n = 4: S_4 = \text{"bab"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-ending-bbb})

\( n \geq 5: S_n[(F_n-1)..F_n] = (S_{n-1}S_{n-2})[(F_n-1)..F_n] = S_{n-2}[(F_{n_2}-1)..F_{n-2}] \neq \text{"bb"} \implies \) (\ref{sec:no-ending-bbb})

\section{No "bbb"}\label{sec:no-bbb}

No "bbb" appears in \( S_n \).

In order to get "bbb" you have have to have

either

- "bb" at the ending of \( S_{n-1} \) and "b" at the beginning of \( S_{n-2} \).

- "b" at the ending of \( S_{n-1} \) and "bb" at the beginning of \( S_{n-2} \).

By sections \ref{sec:no-starting-bbb} and \ref{sec:no-ending-bbb} both cases are impossible.
