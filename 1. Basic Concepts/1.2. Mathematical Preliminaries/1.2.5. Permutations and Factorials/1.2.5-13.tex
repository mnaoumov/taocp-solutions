\( k^{2} = 1 \iff k = \pm 1 \)

\( p - 1 \equiv -1\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \)

\( \forall k, 2 \leq k \leq p - 2: k^{2} \not\equiv 1\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \implies \exists l = l(k), 2 \leq l \leq p - 2, l \neq k: kl \equiv 1\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \)

\( (p-1)! = \prod_{k=1}^{p-1} k = 1 \cdot \prod_{2 \leq k \leq p - 2} k \cdot (p -1) = (p-1) \prod_{\substack{2 \leq k \leq p - 2\\jq \not\equiv 1, 2 \leq j \leq k - 1}}kl(k) \equiv p - 1 \ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \)
