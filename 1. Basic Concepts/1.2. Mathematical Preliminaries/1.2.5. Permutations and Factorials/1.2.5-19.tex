\( \Gamma_{m}(x) = \frac{m^{x} m!}{x(x+1)\cdot\quad\cdots\quad\cdot(x+m)} \)

\( A_{m}(x) := \int_{0}^{m} {(1-\frac{t}{m})}^{m} t^{x-1}dt \overset{t=ms}{=} \int_{0}^{1} {(1-s)}^{m}{(ms)}^{x-1}mds = m^{x} \overbrace{\int_{0}^{1} {(1-s)}^{m}s^{x-1}ds}^{B_{m}(x)} \)

\( C_{m}(x) := \frac{\Gamma_{m}(x)}{m^{x}} = \frac{m!}{x(x+1)\cdot\quad\cdots\quad\cdot(x+m)} \)

Let's prove by induction of \( m: B_{m}(x) = C_{m}(x) \)

\( m = 1: B_{1}(x) = \int_{0}^{1} {(1-s)}^{1} s^{x-1}ds = \int_{0}^{1} (s^{x-1}-s^{x})ds = \frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{x+1} = \frac{1}{x(x+1)} = C_{1}(x) \)

\( (m \implies m + 1) \)
