(d): \( \sum_{R(j)} a_{j} + \sum_{S(j)} a_{j} = \sum_{R(j) \lor S(j)} a_{j} + \sum_{R(j) \land S(j)} a_{j} \)

If we have conditions such that \( R(j) \land S(j) \) are never satisfied

\( \forall j: R(j) \lor S(j) \) precisely either \( R(j) \) or \( S(j) \), but not together

\( \implies \) each summand in \( \sum_{R(j) \lor S(j)} a_{j} \) has exactly one corresponding from \( \sum_{R(j)} a_{j} \) or \( \sum_{S(j)} a_{j} \)

\( \implies \sum_{R(j)} a_{j} + \sum_{S(j)} a_{j} = \sum_{R(j) \lor S(j)} a_{j} \)

\( \implies \sum_{R(j) \land S(j)} a_{j} = 0 \)

Similarly, from 1.2.3-22, \( \prod_{R(j) \land S(j)} a_{j} = 1 \) % chktex 8
