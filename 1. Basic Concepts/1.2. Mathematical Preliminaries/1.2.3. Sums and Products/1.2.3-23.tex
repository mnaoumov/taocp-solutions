(d): \( \sum_{R(j)} a_j + \sum_{S(j)} a_j = \sum_{R(j) \lor S(j)} a_j + \sum_{R(j) \land S(j)} a_j \)

If we have conditions such that \( R(j) \land S(j) \) are never satisfied

\( \forall j: R(j) \lor S(j) \) precisely either \( R(j) \) or \( S(j) \), but not together

\( \implies \) each summand in \( \sum_{R(j) \lor S(j)} a_j \) has exactly one corresponding from \( \sum_{R(j)} a_j \) or \( \sum_{S(j)} a_j \)

\( \implies \sum_{R(j)} a_j + \sum_{S(j)} a_j = \sum_{R(j) \lor S(j)} a_j \)

\( \implies \sum_{R(j) \land S(j)} a_j = 0 \)

Similarly, from [1.2.3-22](<./1.2.3-22.md>), \( \prod_{R(j) \land S(j)} a_j = 1 \)
