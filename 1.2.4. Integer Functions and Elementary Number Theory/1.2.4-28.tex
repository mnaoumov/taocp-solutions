\textbf{Lemma} \( a \perp n, b \perp n \implies (ab) \perp n \)

Let \( d = \gcd(ab, n) \)

Assume \( d > 1 \implies \exists p \) - prime, \( p \backslash d \implies p \backslash (ab), p \backslash n \)

\( a \perp n = 1 \implies a \perp p = 1 \)

\( ab \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \)

\( \implies b \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \implies p \backslash b \implies p \backslash \gcd(b,n) \implies b \not\perp n \) - Contradiction

\bigskip\hrule\bigskip

\( 0 \leq x_1 < x_2 < \ldots < x_{\varphi(m)} \leq m - 1, x_k \perp m, 1 \leq k \leq \varphi(m) \)

\( y_k := (ax_k) \mod m, 1 \leq k \leq \varphi(m) \)

\( a \perp m, x_k \perp m \overset{\text{Lemma}}{\implies} (ax_k) \perp m \implies y_k \perp m \)

\( y_k = y_l \implies ax_k \equiv ax_l \ (\mathrm{modulo}\ m) \implies x_k \equiv x_l\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \implies x_k = x_l \)

\( k \neq l \implies y_k \neq y_l \)

\( \implies \{ y_1, y_2, \ldots, y_{\varphi(m)} \} = \{ x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_{\varphi(m)} \} \)

\( x_1x_2 \cdot\ \cdots\ \cdot x_m = y_1y_2\cdots y_m \equiv (ax_1)(ax_2) \cdot\ \cdots\ \cdot (ax_m) = a^{\varphi(m)}(x_1x_2 \cdot\ \cdots\ \cdot x_m)\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ m) \)

\( x_1 \perp m \implies x_2 \cdots x_m \equiv a^{\varphi(m)}(x_2\cdots x_m)\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ m) \)

\( \vdots \)

\( 1 \equiv a^{\varphi(m)} \ (\mathrm{modulo}\ m) \)
