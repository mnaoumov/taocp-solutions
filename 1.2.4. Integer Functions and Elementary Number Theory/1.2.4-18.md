# 1.2.4-18

${} a \equiv b\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ r), a \equiv b\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ s), r \perp s {}$

${} a - b \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ r) \implies a - b = kr {}$

${} a - b \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ s) \implies kr \equiv 0\cdot r \ (\mathrm{modulo}\ s) {}$

Law B ${} \implies k \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ s) \implies k = ls {}$

${} a - b = kr = lsr \implies a - b \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ rs) \implies a \equiv b\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ rs) {}$
