# 1.2.4-25

Theorem F: ${} a^{p} \equiv a\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) {}$

If ${} p \backslash a \implies a \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \implies a^{p-1} = a \cdot a^{p-2} \equiv 0 \cdot a^{p-2} \equiv 0\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \implies a^{p-1} \mod p = 0 = [\lnot(p \backslash a)] {}$

If ${} \lnot(p \backslash a) \implies p \perp a {}$

${} a \cdot a^{p-1} \equiv a \cdot 1\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \implies a^{p-1}\equiv 1\ (\mathrm{modulo}\ p) \implies a^{p-1} \mod p = 1 = [\lnot(p \backslash a)] {}$
